## Problem

Revenue leakage rarely announces itself. It accumulates through unauthorized discounts, informal approvals, unvalidated pricing, and no real-time visibility into margin. For AI-driven forecasting, anomaly detection, or decision support to be trustworthy, the underlying financial data must be accurate, governed, and traceable. In this environment:

- Discounts and approvals were not consistently logged.
- Pricing logic lived in spreadsheets and tribal knowledge.
- Margin was only visible after the month closed.

Profitability and AI insights cannot be protected after the fact; they have to be enforced and observed at the point of every transaction.
  </div>
  <div>
## Solution

I designed a financial governance engine that applies MLOps-style operational rigor to revenue, cost, and margin. Profitability is protected through system-enforced controls: every discount, approval, cost, and payout is validated, logged, and traceable from pipeline to delivery. The result is an AI-ready financial backbone where every transaction passes through automated control gates, margin is computed in real time, and structured data can support AI models, anomaly detection, and intelligent decision support.

## How It Works

<HowItWorksGrid>
<HowItWorksCard title="Live Margin Computation & Telemetry">
Client revenue, estimated vendor costs, actual costs, dollar variance, and margin percentage are computed automatically on every project and updated in real time, creating continuous financial telemetry for AI models, anomaly detectors, or optimization agents.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="Pricing Bands, Guardrails & Floor Enforcement">
Min and max price-per-unit are defined on both sell and cost sides. Line-item quantity floors trigger inline warnings before a quote is finalized, creating machine-readable pricing logic for AI pricing assistants or recommendation engines.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="Governed Financial Lifecycle & Audit Trail">
Every monetary record moves through governed states with transitions enforced by business rules. Each state change logs a timestamped audit entry with user and previous state for explainability, compliance reviews, and anomaly investigations.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="Role-Based Financial Permissions">
Read, write, create, and delete permissions are defined per model and security group. Sensitive data such as margins, costs, and compensation is hidden from unauthorized roles so AI-enabled workflows operate inside strict access boundaries.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="Post-Delivery Cost Accountability & Variance Signals">
Every vendor assignment tracks estimated vs. actual cost with auto-computed variance. Overruns surface on the assignment, project, and live margin calculation, forming signals for anomaly detection and forecasting error analysis.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="End-to-End Budget-to-Actual Chain">
Budget is captured at pipeline, priced on the sales order, and measured against actuals at delivery. This linked financial lineage supports AI forecasting models, profitability analysis, and scenario planning.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="Automated Payment Eligibility Gate">
Four conditions must clear before any payout processes: delivery confirmed, all customer invoices paid, all vendor bills paid, and margin threshold met. This control gate prevents AI or rule-based automations from triggering payouts outside governance rules.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="7-Tier Compensation Engine">
Rates scale with gross margin performance from 0% below the floor to maximum rate at high-margin delivery. The formula, (Revenue x Attribution %) x Margin Tier Rate, is codified for incentive simulation, payout forecasting, and behavior analysis.
</HowItWorksCard>

<HowItWorksCard title="Automated Compensation Records & Traceability">
Draft purchase orders are auto-generated monthly per stakeholder, with line-level tracking codes linking each payout to its source project. This creates a traceable compensation dataset for performance analytics and incentive optimization.
</HowItWorksCard>
</HowItWorksGrid>
